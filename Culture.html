Where It All Started

Alternative rock first came from the independent music underground of the 1980's. And over time eventually became widely popular in the 1990's with groups such as Green Day, Nirvana,Coldplay, Linkin Park, and Blink-182. Nirvana was the main group to have the biggest effect on Alternative rock by making it go from the underground to where millions started to enjoy the genre.

Green Day

Green Day was originally part of the Punk scene at the DIY 924 Gilman Street Club in Berkely, California. They made their major debut in 1994, when a major label (Reprise Records) released their album "Dookie". They recieved very good feedback when they eventually shipped over 10 million copies in the U.S.

Nirvana

In the beginning of Nirvana's career as a band they were established as part the Seattle grunge scene. At that time they released their first album "Bleach" for the independent record label sub pop in 1989. They eventually found their sound of music based on the dynamic contrasts usually between quiet verses and loud, heavy choruses. They found unexpected success with "Smells Like Teen Spirit", their first single a part of their second album "Nevermind". With this success they managed to widely popularize Alternative Rock as a whole. Nirvana's frontman (Cobain) was soon referred to as the "spokesman of a generation" with Nirvana as a whole considered the "flagship band" of Generation X.
